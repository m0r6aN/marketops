# MarketOps Capability Priorities

This assessment uses the internal all-in-one product posture and prioritizes the first go-to-market work for Keon Systems and BioStack. “Immediate” means it improves the next discovery, conversion, content, or measurement loop without requiring a large new platform dependency.

## A. Immediately useful

| Capability | Why now |
|---|---|
| Customer finder and outreach | Directly builds the first Keon and BioStack prospect cohorts; implemented as an evidence-backed finder and planning workflow. |
| Run full marketing campaign | Useful now as an operator-led lifecycle spanning brief, audience, assets, outreach plan, execution status, and measurement. Provider-backed autonomous execution can arrive incrementally. |
| Generate brand voice guidelines | Creates reusable initiative-specific constraints for every content surface. |
| Draft marketing content / write engaging content | High-frequency work across web, email, social, and sales follow-up. These should share one source-backed content workflow. |
| Apply persuasion psychology | Useful as an explicit copy review lens when it improves clarity and relevance without manipulation or unsupported claims. |
| Short-form video script writer | Converts existing product proof and education into a reusable distribution format before adding video-generation infrastructure. |
| Grab YouTube transcripts | Low-complexity research and content-repurposing input, subject to source rights and platform terms. |
| Email marketing campaign builder | Immediately useful for audience, sequence, subject, CTA, and measurement planning even before automated delivery. |
| SEO / AEO / GEO audit | Finds concrete discoverability gaps in the two live product surfaces. |
| Optimize for AI citations | Useful when treated as source clarity, answerability, structured evidence, and authoritative-page improvement—not a promise of citation placement. |

## B. Useful, but not quite yet

| Capability | What should precede it |
|---|---|
| Classify and prioritize emails | Connect after there is enough real inbox volume and stable product-specific routing labels. |
| Explainer video creation | Start after scripts, brand voice, proof assets, and a repeatable distribution plan exist. |
| Brand kit to video | Useful once each product has a stable brand kit and the video workflow has proven demand. |
| Automate CRM workflows | Add after the customer record, stages, ownership, and conversion events are stable inside MarketOps. |
| Fix user retention drop-off | High value after BioStack or Keon has reliable product telemetry and a meaningful cohort to diagnose. |
| Publish to all social platforms | Add platforms in priority order after content review, account ownership, cadence, and attribution are working on one or two channels. |
| Blue Ocean Strategy planner | Useful as periodic positioning support, but less urgent than live customer evidence and campaign learning. |
| Launch SEO / AEO / GEO pages at scale | Start with a small number of high-quality templates and measured pages before scaling. |

## C. Useful, but only if…

| Capability | Condition |
|---|---|
| Digital clone / avatar like HeyGen | Only with explicit likeness consent, durable asset rights, disclosure rules, and a real distribution use case. |
| Animate face with audio | Only with consent, source rights, provenance, and clear labeling that prevents deceptive use. |
| Animate a still image | Only when the source image may legally be transformed and the output supports a defined campaign. |
| Voice cloning like ElevenLabs | Only with recorded speaker consent, vendor rights, secure voice-asset handling, and disclosure appropriate to the channel. |
| Turn text into voice | Only after a channel needs audio and pronunciation, accessibility, brand voice, and licensing requirements are defined. |
| Email automation | Only after sender authentication, consent or another lawful basis, unsubscribe handling, suppression lists, rate limits, and provider APIs are configured. |
| Uses Keon Runtime, Collective, and Cortex | Only through current integration contracts where each component has a concrete job; do not couple basic MarketOps workflows to their availability. |
| Make AI phone calls | Only with jurisdiction-aware consent, recording disclosure, do-not-call handling, identity transparency, escalation, and human ownership. |
| Patent search | Only as prior-art research support with source provenance and clear non-legal-advice labeling. |
| Patent countdown | Only if MarketOps intentionally expands into an IP-operations module with verified dates and attorney ownership. |

## D. Probably not useful in core MarketOps

| Capability | Better home |
|---|---|
| Write investment memo | A finance, fundraising, or portfolio-analysis workspace; MarketOps may supply market evidence but should not own the memo workflow. |
| Assess legal contract risk | A dedicated legal review product with counsel-approved playbooks; MarketOps can link the result. |
| File provisional and full patent applications | Attorney-led IP operations. Repository automation cannot replace inventorship review, disclosure quality, filing authorization, or legal advice. |

## External constraints the repository cannot remove

- Data sources and enrichment providers impose licenses, terms of service, privacy requirements, rate limits, and permitted-use rules.
- Email, social, and CRM providers control account access, APIs, anti-abuse rules, deliverability, and platform-specific automation limits.
- Outreach may be subject to privacy, anti-spam, telemarketing, recording-consent, and do-not-call laws based on the sender, recipient, channel, and jurisdiction.
- Avatar, voice, image, transcript, and video workflows require consent and intellectual-property rights that software cannot infer.
- Patent filing deadlines and legal sufficiency require verified facts and qualified legal ownership; a countdown or generated draft is not authoritative.
